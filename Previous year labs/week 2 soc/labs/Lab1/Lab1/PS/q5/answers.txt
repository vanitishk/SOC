OLS vs. Regularization Behavior: Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) struggles heavily with multicollinearity. When features are highly correlated, OLS becomes unstable and often assigns wildly different or opposing weights to them. Ridge regularization uses an L2 penalty, which penalizes the squared magnitude of the coefficients. Mathematically, distributing the weight evenly across two correlated features results in a much lower overall penalty than assigning all the weight to just one.
Implication: Because Ridge forces their paths to converge, it implies these two features are highly redundant and are measuring the exact same underlying signal. The correlation coefficient between them is exceptionally high at [Insert Correlation Number, e.g., 0.98].
